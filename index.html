PORK ADOBO

INGREDIENTS

  • 1.5 lbs (about 0.7 kg) pork shoulder or belly,cut into 1.5-inch cubes
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup white vinegar
  • 5-6 cloves garlic, crushed or minced
  • 3 bay leaves, dried
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar(optional, for balance)
  • Salt to taste(optional)

PROCEDURE

    Marination (Optional, but Recommended)
  1. In a large bowl, combine the pork cubes, soy sauce, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Mix well.
  2. Cover the bowl and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.
    Cooking
  1. Sear the pork
    Heat the cooking oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the marinated pork pieces (reserving the marinade liquid) and cook, stirring occasionally, until the pork is lightly browned on all sides.
  2. Add braising liquid
    Pour in the reserved marinade and the water. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Simmer until tender
    Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork is fork-tender.
  4. Add vinegar and reduce sauce
    Pour in the vinegar, but do not stir immediately; let it re-boil for a few minutes to cook off the strong acid taste. Stir everything together and continue to simmer, uncovered, for another 10 to 15 minutes or until the sauce has reduced to a slightly thickened glaze and the pork has absorbed the flavor.
  5. Final seasoning
    Taste and adjust seasoning with sugar or a little salt if needed.
  6. Serve
    Remove the bay leaves and transfer the pork adobo to a serving dish.
